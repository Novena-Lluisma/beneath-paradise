🌊 Ocean is Changing

Even a small rise in sea surface temperature can disrupt coral reefs, threaten marine biodiversity, and reshape ocean ecosystems across the Pacific. This visualization presents long-term Sea Surface Temperature (SST) trends for Pacific Island countries from 1990 to 2025.

About this Indicator

Sea Surface Temperature (SST) measures the average temperature of the ocean surface. Changes in SST can influence coral reefs, marine biodiversity, and ecosystem health.

🌊 Rising Seas

Even small increases in sea level can accelerate coastal flooding, shoreline erosion, and threaten low-lying Pacific islands. Explore how sea level anomalies have changed over time.

About this Indicator

Sea Level Anomalies measure changes in sea level relative to the long-term average. Rising sea levels increase coastal flooding, erosion, and saltwater intrusion, especially for low-lying Pacific islands.

🌿 Red List Index

The Red List Index reflects changes in overall biodiversity. Higher values indicate healthier ecosystems, while declining values suggest increasing extinction risk for species.

🌏 Final Reflection

Climate change affects the Pacific through rising sea levels and declining biodiversity. Protecting marine ecosystems and reducing global emissions are essential to safeguard the future of Pacific Island communities.

🌊 Rising Seas

Sea level anomalies have generally increased across many Pacific Island countries, raising the risk of coastal flooding, erosion, and saltwater intrusion.

🐠 Biodiversity

The Red List Index shows gradual declines in biodiversity, highlighting growing extinction risks and the need for stronger conservation efforts.

🌱 Call to Action

Sustainable policies, ecosystem restoration, and global cooperation are crucial to protect the Pacific for future generations.
